# Visual Design Manifesto: PC-98 東方 Project Aesthetics

This document serves as a permanent design blueprint for TimeBox Vibe's visual and audio identity.

## Principles

### 1. Reject Modern Corporate Monotony
Modern user interfaces have been flattened and drained of color, relying on sterile grayscale grids, massive rounded borders, and smooth animations. We reject this. Our application has a soul, built on the vibrant, highly saturated, and tinted dark palettes of early 90s PC-98 Japanese personal computer games.

### 2. Reject Simple Blocky "Minecraft" Pixel Art
Retro pixel art does not mean drawing chunky, simple squares with flat borders. Authentic PC-98 graphics were complex, utilizing double-concentric lines, dithered shading, and ornate traditional decorative corners (reminiscent of shrine architecture and magical sigils). 

### 3. Hardware Constraints as Art
We embrace the technical limitations of 90s hardware:
- **Instant Snapping**: Color transitions and tab selections snap instantly.
- **Procedural Math Graphics**: Rather than static assets, UI symbols (Danmaku fireballs, Ofuda talismans, Yin-Yang orbs) are rendered dynamically pixel-by-pixel using clean math routines.
- **PSG Chiptune Audio**: High-energy chiptune sound waves are synthesized in real-time with ADSR envelopes and authentic chord progressions.
